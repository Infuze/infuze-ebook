Type of vegetable protein

Tempeh

Description of the vegetable protein:

Tempeh is made by fermenting soybeans in banana leaves until a firm, earthy patty forms. Tempeh contains more protein and dietary fibre than traditional tofu, and its mild nutty flavour works great in a wide array of recipes.

Type of vegetable protein

Seitan

Description of the vegetable protein:

Seitan, also known as wheat gluten, is the product of rinsing and cooking wheat dough to remove the starch, leaving a protein-dense substance that turned out to be an excellent meat substitute/alternative. It can be used to create delicious mock meats.

Tofu - both firm and soft

Description of the vegetable protein:

Tofu is also known as bean or soya curd, tofu is a soft, cheese-like food made of condensed soy milk pressed into tight blocks and mixed with nigari. While this product can be rather bland, tofu easily absorbs flavours of other ingredients, such as spices.
