Large pan with a tight fitting lid and a perforated spoon to stir the mussels.
Grilled trout
Cutting boards (coloured for seafood only), knives to make incisions for faster cooking, baking tray.

Area Description
Customer Example: What type of seafood they are likely to order: whole fish, fillets, shellfish? What style of service is it?
Portion size and cuts
Portion size required by the establishment; the cuts needed, for example goujons or cutlets; and the amounts that you are likely to sell as the seafood will go off quicker once cut and handled.
Hygiene
Maintain the quality and freshness by correct storage. Clean the seafood according to enterprise and hygiene standards.
Accompaniments
Part of the mise en place, for example vegetables, mushrooms, noodles, rice, herbs or potatoes.
Sauces
Type of sauce served with the dish – creamy sauces are more filling hence a smaller serving size of fish is sufficient.
Marinades or pastes
Worcestershire or soy sauce, chermoula or curry paste as part of mise en place.
Garnishes
Part of the mise en place, for example lemon wedges, herbs, grilled tomatoes, parsnip chips etc.

Dish Suitable garnishes and accompaniments
Deep-fried cod in batter
Tartare sauce is served for deep-fried seafood. Remoulade and gribiche are other popular sauces. Lemon served as wedges or lemon halves wrapped in muslin cloth. Fried chipped potatoes are standard for fish and chips. Deep-fried parsley sprig.
Fish meunière

Cold-smoked salmon
Lemon served as wedges or lemon halves wrapped in muslin cloth. Sliced onions, capers and hard-boiled eggs, brown bread, cracked black pepper and dill sprigs.
Pan-fried trout
Lemon peeled and sliced, nut butter sauce (beurre noisette), chopped parsley.
Moules marinières
French-fried potatoes, chopped parsley.
Poached cod
Chopped parsley usually served with a white sauce such as parsley.
