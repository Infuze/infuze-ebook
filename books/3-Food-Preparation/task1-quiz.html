Greasing

Greasing is the process of adding grease in the form of oil or fat, to stop food from sticking to the pan or tray when baked. This is important when prepared items have a low fat content in the mixture or item, as the fat contained within the dish wonâ€™t leach out and it will be prone to sticking to the baking tray.

Lining

Lining is the process of lining paper between metal surfaces and prepared foods so that the foods do not stick to baking sheets, pie tins, muffin tins, baking dishes and other surfaces after baking.

Marking

Marking is the process of adding lines or marks to a baked dish before baking. For instance, you could mark a large pie by gently scoring the top with a knife or a pastry wheel before baking.

Humidity can have an effect on how food bakes. Humidity is something that traditional ovens (with no steam inject) have no control over. Water molecules conduct heat much faster than air. Not only does humid air transfer heat more efficiently to food, cooking it more quickly, it also helps prevent the water in the food from evaporating, keeping the moisture locked inside.

In a traditional oven there will be different parts of the oven which will operate at different temperatures, even if the temperature of the oven is set. This is because cold air in the oven will sink to the bottom and the hotter air will rise to the top. This means that the top shelves of the oven will be hotter than the bottom shelf of the ovens. During the baking process, you may be required to move items between the shelves to achieve even baking.
