QUESTION 1

Explain the following terms used when roasting.

Enter your answers in the textbox below then check by clicking 'REVEAL'

Barding

Barding is a method of placing fat (such as bacon or strips of pork fat) around lean meats or poultry that are to be roasted, so they will absorb additional moisture and fat to keep them from drying out. The barded fat bastes the meat while it is roasting, adding flavour to the meat, especially on lean cuts which lack sufficient fat to remain moist and succulent.

Searing

Searing is the process of quickly browning the surface of food by exposing it to extremely high heat. The item is most often seared in a small quantity of oil and is then roasted in an oven.

Trussing

Trussing or tying various cuts of meat and poultry is a good way to hold the shape of a roast, to keep the meat in a compact form, and to make sure that the meat will be cooked evenly. The tying allows the roast to be stuffed and makes for a more attractive presentation when serving.
