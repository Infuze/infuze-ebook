QUESTION 1

Explain the following terms used when braising.

Covering

Some dishes may require the food item to be covered to keep in the heat and flavours and stop them from evaporating. Covering with a lid or tin foil will form a ceiling that helps to keep in the moisture and prevents the top of the food item from drying out when placed in the oven. This helps to ensure that the finished stewed food item is moist throughout.

Draining

Draining is the process of pouring off the excess cooking liquid from food; for example, draining the excess cooking liquid/liquor from a braised joint of meat. The cooking liquor/liquid can then be reduced/thickened and made into a sauce.

Sufficient liquid

It is important when braising products that there is sufficient liquid for the food to cook properly. The pan used should be just large enough so that any liquid can freely move around the product. It shouldn’t have too much liquid compared to the size of the product being braised.

It is very important at all stages of handling, preparation and cooking of foods for braising that the temperature of the food is monitored to avoid the multiplication of harmful bacteria. Prior to cooking, all foods for braising should be stored in the correct cold refrigeration at a temperature of 3–8°C until required. QUESTION 3

Explain the following terms used when stewing.

Blanching

Blanching is the cooking of food in boiling water for only a short amount of time to retain the colour and to help preserve the nutrients. The procedure involves placing small quantities of food into a large amount of rapidly boiling water for the amount of time specified in the recipe.

Searing

Searing is the process of quickly browning the surface of food by exposing it to extremely high heat. When used in stewing, the food is most often seared in a small quantity of oil, and is then added with cooking liquid/liquor and vegetables and stewed for a long period of time.

Reducing

A reduction of wine, stock or other liquid can be added to a stew to give an intense base flavour. It is the process of thickening and intensifying the flavour of a liquid by rapidly boiling the contents in an uncovered pan in order to evaporate excess liquid.
