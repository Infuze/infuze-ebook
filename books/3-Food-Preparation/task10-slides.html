Evaluation

  • It is important that as a chef you are able to measure and evaluate your dish against quality standards throughout preparation, cooking and service.
  • You must also be able recognise any faults and know the correct procedures to deal with them.
  • The quality standard may include producing the dishes to the correct specification of the recipe cards and the agreed standard expected of the establishment.

Evaluation

Always check the finished dish before it is served to the customer. Never serve a food item that doesn't meet the requirements or the standard!

Always check through out all stages of cooking and serving. Remember somebody is paying for the food.

Performing a sensory evaluation

Every dish is made up of an ideal combination of elements that we can sense:

  • Appearance
  • Taste
  • Aroma
  • Texture
  • Consistency.

But you should also check the following:

  • Portion size
  • Colour
  • Presentation
  • Temperature.

Evaluating portion size and colour

Portion size

  • Does the size reflect the dish/recipe/menu/course?
  • Is the portion size suitable for any accompaniments?

Colour

  • Is the meat correctly coloured to meet the requirements of the dish?
  • Does it have too much or too little colour?
  • Does it need longer cooking to achieve the desired finished colour?
  • Is it evenly coloured?

Evaluating taste and texture

Taste

  • Is it correctly seasoned?
  • Does it taste like it is supposed too?
  • Are there any overpowering flavours?
  • Do I need to adjust any of the flavouring?

Texture

  • Is it overcooked? Is it undercooked?
  • Does the outside need to be crispy?
  • Is the inside of the product moist?
  • Is it evenly cooked throughout?

Evaluating consistency and presentation

Consistency

  • Is the dish at the correct consistency?
  • Are any sauces too thick or too thin?
  • Is there consistency between the same dishes served to different customers?

Presentation

  • Does it look appealing to the customer?
  • Is it correctly garnished?
  • Is it served with the correct sauce or accompaniment?
  • Is it served on the correct service equipment?

Evaluating temperature and aroma

Temperature

  • Has it met the legal temperature requirement?
  • Is it too hot or too cold?
  • Has the dish been reheated?

Aroma

  • Does the dish smell good?
  • Are there any unfamiliar or bad smells?

Fault reporting

  • At all stages throughout preparation, cooking and service if you find a problem with the ingredients, equipment or the dish you must report it to your supervisor immediately. Alternatively you must know the correct procedures to deal with it in a safe and efficient manner.
  • If you do not recognise faults or know the correct procedure to deal with the problem, it may lead to the dish being inedible or the equipment being broken. This can have a financial impact on the business.
