QUESTION 2

The geography of the world has an impact on the supply of food. This includes soil, climate, lakes and terrain. Briefly describe the effects each of these have.

Enter your answers in the editable boxes below then check by clicking 'REVEAL'

Now check your answers to the correct answers shown below.

Geographical feature Effect
Soil
Soil provides nutrients for vegetables, fruit and other organic foods. If there are insufficient amounts of nutrients, then the growth of the plant will be restricted.
Climate
Climate probably has the largest effect on food. This includes providing sunlight and warmth for growing organic food, and rainfall – not enough rain and plants and animals may not grow.
Lakes, rivers and seas
Communities close to a lake or river, or surrounded by the sea, tend to have an abundance of fish. Lakes and rivers also provide a source of water for agriculture.
Terrain
The terrain can best be defined as the natural features of an area of land. Some commodities need good drainage, others plenty of water, more shade than sunlight etc.
