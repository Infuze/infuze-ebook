QUESTION 3

Describe how the following quality points of meat determine its eating quality.

Enter your answers in the editable boxes below then check by clicking 'REVEAL'

Now check your answers to the correct answers shown below.

Quality points Descriptions
Example: Breed Some breeds like Brahman beef are naturally tougher than others such as Charolais or St Gertrudis
Diet
If the animal was on a poor diet the meat will be tougher. Grain-fed animals are higher in intra-muscular fat.
Muscle usage
The more a muscle is used the tougher it gets. Consider this when choosing cookery methods. For example shank is braised to tenderise collagen.
Stress
If the animal is stressed during slaughter it will darken in colour and become tougher as the pH is affected.
Age
The younger an animal is the more tender it is in general.
Grading
Different grading regimes apply but most are based on meat colour, fat colour, internal fat etc.
Fat coverage and colour
The amount of fat and its colour are a quality indicator. For example, with beef yellow fat indicates often an older animal.

QUESTION 4

Describe how the following quality points of offal determine its eating quality.

Enter your answers in the editable boxes below then check by clicking 'REVEAL'

Now check your answers to the correct answers shown below.

Quality points Descriptions
Example: Bone size Smaller bones indicate younger, usually more tender animals. This is helpful when selecting oxtail or marrow bones
Smell
Good quality, clean, fresh offal should have a clean smell. For example, kidney should not smell too much of urine.
Texture
There should be a firm texture without tears or cuts. For example, if liver is too porous it indicates older animal.
Colour
The colour should be indicative of the type of animal. For example, liver of beef will be darker than veal liver.
Fat colour
The fat colour of various organs should be indicative of the type of animal. For example, kidney of beef will be darker than veal kidney.
Size
Smaller size generally indicates younger animal
