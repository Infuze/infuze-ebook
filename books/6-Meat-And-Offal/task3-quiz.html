Roasting is used extensively for meat and poultry. Whole primal cuts are cooked either bone-in or boned out and are often stuffed and tied. Place on a trivet or bones and cook to the desired degree of doneness. 45–60 minutes per kg.

Baking is popular for primal or portion cuts of meat and offal. The item can be sealed, enveloped in pastry, for example puff, filo or brioche and then baked. Pies are a popular item that use this method. Also items such as pork or beef wellington.

All first-class portion cuts are suitable and should be placed on the grill and turned once the meat surface starts to show small beads of blood. Then continue cooking until the beads appear again for a medium rare product.

All second-class primal cuts of meat are suitable for braising. The meat is sealed and placed in a pot or braisière on mirepoix. Stock is added to come just halfway up the meat. Add a lid and cook until done.
