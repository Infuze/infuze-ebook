Name of item

Corer

Description of the item and its usage

Round bladed speciality tool for removing the cores and seeds of apples, pears and cucumbers. Corer may be v-shaped or formed into a half circle or full circled that has scooped sides.

Name of item

Turning knife

Description of the item and its usage

A shorter blade than a paring knife with a blade that is typically 2 to 3 inch long. It is designed to curve upward on both the cutting edge and the top edge. The turning knife is commonly used to turn vegetables.

Name of item

Sharpening steel

Description of the item and its usage

A tool generally made of steel, which is used to realign the knife's edge. Running it across the steel will straighten the edge and the knife will be sharp again.

Name of item

Filleting knife

Description of the item and its usage

A knife consisting of a thin flexible blade, typically 6 to 11 inches long, used for filleting fish. The narrow blade enables the knife to cleanly move along the backbones of the fish, in and around areas adjacent to bones, and to evenly slice along the skin, removing it easily from the flesh.

Name of item

Palette knife

Description of the item and its usage

A small/large knife with a blunt edge blade that is used to apply creams and spreads.

Name of item

Serrated knife

Description of the item and its usage

A knife with a sharp edge that has saw-like notches or teeth. The blade of a serrated knife is 5 to 10 inches long. A serrated knife with a long blade is used to slice through food that is hard on the outside and soft on the inside.
