When boiling rice ensure you use a high ratio of water – 10:1, then drain well, refresh and store on trays and cover. Place in the fridge and use within 1–2 days. Where possible avoid reheating rice.

Grains can be boiled, braised, stewed, pan fried or steamed. When used for stuffing, baking is also suitable. Pre-cooked grains can be reheated over steam, in the microwave or tossed with butter.

Dried pasta
When boiling pasta, use a ratio of 10 litres salted water to 1kg pasta and pour some oil on top to prevent the pasta sticking together.
Most dried pasta will need about 12 minutes of cooking time as the dried pasta needs to soften first. Always check the packaging for suggested cooking times.
Fresh pasta
When boiling pasta, use a ratio of 10 litres salted water to 1kg pasta and pour some oil on top to prevent the pasta sticking together
Fresh pasta should only be cooked for 2–3 minutes to be ‘al dente’ meaning it is cooked but still retains some firmness. Use fresh pasta as soon as possible or freeze it.
Reheating cooked pasta
Reheat the pasta in boiling salt water or special pasta cookers. Alternatively, toss through in a pan with oil or butter or use the microwave.
