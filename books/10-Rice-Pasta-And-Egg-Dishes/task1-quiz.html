  • Polished rice (white rice) has the outer husks removed and has no traces of rice flour.
  • Brown rice contains the bran, has a nutty flavour and consistency and is more nutritious. It takes longer to cook as the husk needs to soften.
  • Parboiled rice has been pre-cooked, which thickens the starch and provides firmer rice that does not stick together. It may take a bit longer to cook than white rice.
  • Short-grain and medium-grain rice has a rounder-shaped grain that absorbs more moisture as the outer starch or amylopectin is broken down gently as the liquid is absorbed.
  • Red rice is a fermented rice made in Asia that is soaked and mixed with yeast monascus purpureus which gives it a rich red colour. The French Camargue rice is a short-grain variety that has a natural red pigment in the husk.
  • Black rice is a very glutinous rice often used for sweet dishes. The black colour turns into a rich purple, combining well with coconut milk used in the recipe.
  • Wild rice is an aquatic grass harvested in North America, with a firm outer layer and a chewy texture once cooked. It will expand considerably and is often mixed with other rice due to its cost.

QUESTION 2

What are the characteristics of the following types of grains?

Semolina

Semolina is the coarsely milled endosperm of durum wheat which may be used for pasta production but also in breakfast cereals, dumplings, puddings and desserts. Durum wheat semolina has a richer yellow colour whilst softer flours have whiter grains.

Couscous

Couscous is a North African cereal dish made from semolina or other grains such as millet that are moistened and coated with flour and then dried. Varieties are available from the small-grained Moroccan types to the larger moghrabieh and ptitim.

Cracked wheat and bulgur

Cracked wheat and bulgur – these two are often confused. Bulgur refers to a par-boiled wheat grain that has been cracked; whilst cracked wheat is not parboiled and must be steamed to be palatable.

Tapioca

Tapioca is made from the cassava plant and most commonly purchased as tapioca pearls. These are smaller than sago but have the same property of thickening roughly twice their weight. Both sago and tapioca are perfect for gluten-free diets as they do not contain any gluten.

Polenta

Polenta is based on corn (maize) meal. Originally polenta required cooking for several hours before instant polenta became available

Rye

Rye grain is stronger flavoured than wheat and is often used in baking for more solid type breads. Rye has a stronger flavour and is high in dietary fibre, calcium iron and zinc.

Buckwheat

Buckwheat is not wheat and is a soft grain that can be used for soba noodles or produced into a batter for blinis, classical small pancakes served with caviar.

Oats

Oats are a popular breakfast cereal available in various sizes of flakes.

Quinoa

Quinoa is a South American grain that has excellent nutritional benefits. It has the highest protein content of any grain and is rich in vitamins and minerals and fibre.
