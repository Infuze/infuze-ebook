QUESTION 3

Describe what are the differences between instant and soaking marinades?

Enter your answers in the text box below then check by clicking 'REVEAL'

Instant marinades

Instant marinades are used for items which are prepared within a short time period e.g. 20 minutes to 24 hours, using tender items. Ingredients used include: Lemon juice, Worcestershire sauce, soy sauce, yoghurt and tandoori paste, herbs and oil

Soaking marinades

Soaking marinades are used for older birds. Items are often marinated for several days before cooking or smoking. Ingredients include butter milk, red wine, port, brines, pine branches, juniper berries, rosemary and other strong flavours.
