QUESTION 1

The overall aims of any food business are to provide safe food and to make a profit. One way in which this can be achieved is by supervisors supporting the development of their chefs. Briefly describe each of the development types below.

Shadowing
Shadowing is all about an employee observing someone doing a job and then trying to understand how it is done. This is a good, cost-efficient training tool to provide a standardised approach that a business undertakes.
Coaching
An employee who is tasked with coaching/demonstrating tasks to another, probably new, employee has many roles to fill. These include demonstrating new skills, assessing and providing feedback, facilitating training and acting as a role model.
Qualifications
The provision of qualifications is an essential tool in the development of chefs in a professional kitchen. This can be seen UK-wide, with the focus on the delivery of apprenticeships as a means to upgrade and upskill the national workforce.
Mentoring
Mentoring is all about the person who the activity is aimed at – the mentee. It is built around a relationship between the two people involved and, in a practical sense, the mentor is there to guide the mentee without giving all the answers.

QUESTION 4

There are many benefits to being an effective team – the foremost being meeting customer expectations and needs. There are also benefits to the kitchen and the individual. Describe these 3 benefits.

Enter your answers in the textbox below then check by clicking 'REVEAL'

Maximum effectiveness:

Team members who are working together, striving to meet targets and looking out for each other maximise the effectiveness of individuals and the team.

Maximising productivity:

Effective teams that communicate well, achieve targets, work together and focus on the team aims and objectives will maximise their performance and productivity.

Inspiring individuals and teams:

Building the culture of mutual respect and trust allows the free passage of information and skills to each member of the team. An effective supervisor will ensure all team members are inspired to improve their own and the team’s overall delivery.
