QUESTION 1

Describe the following preparation methods and provide an example product for when they are used.

Whisking

The flour batter method employs whisking to combine the eggs and sugar to form a foam. Other ingredients are then added to this mix. It is used to make a smooth paste for almond biscuits.

Creaming

This is used when the fat and sugar are creamed together then beaten. Eggs are then added followed by flour. An example that employs this method is a Victoria sponge.

Folding

This is the technique used to incorporate an ingredient with others that have already been aerated. An example of this is the folding in of the flour when making a Swiss roll or madeleines.

Rolling

This is used to make an even thickness when producing biscuits and cakes to a required thickness. Examples that employ this method include scones and shortbreads.
