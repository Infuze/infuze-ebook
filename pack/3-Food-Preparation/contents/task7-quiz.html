Describe the poaching process.

Add another answer

Now check your answers above to the correct answers below.

Poaching is a process of cooking foods such as eggs, fish or meat in a liquid that has been heated to just below the boiling point, so the liquid is barely moving while the food is being cooked.
A small amount of liquid is added to a pan containing the food so that the food being poached is fully covered with the liquid, but only just.

QUESTION 4

Explain why it is important to drain food after poaching.

Add another answer

Now check your answers above to the correct answers below.

Draining is the process of pouring off the excess cooking liquid from food, such as draining the excess water from poached eggs, vegetables or fish. If the excess liquid is not removed it can make the final product soggy in texture, or the product may still continue to cook in the excess water.
