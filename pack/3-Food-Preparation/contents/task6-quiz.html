QUESTION 1

Explain the following terms used when roasting.

Batting out

Some items, such as meat or poultry, may need to be batted out to reduce their thickness and ensure quick, even cooking. This process also helps to reduce the toughness of meat fibres in a cut of meat or poultry by tenderizing the meat. Tenderizing breaks down the meat fibres and softens the meat, making it easier to chew and more palatable.

Marinating

Marinating is the process of soaking or marinating food items in a flavoured liquid for the purpose of flavouring and tenderizing before cooking. The marinade should totally cover the meat in order for it to work effectively.

Oiling/buttering

Food is oiled or buttered to achieve flavour and colour during the grilling process and to stop it from sticking to the grill pan or tray. This helps to moisten and also colour the item under the direct heat.
