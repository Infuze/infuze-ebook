  • A variety of cuts are used for poultry. Due to its size poultry is often cooked whole.
  • Medium and small birds such as spatchcock, quail or squab can also be flattened for grilling.
  • Duck legs can be used whole e.g. when used in confit or braised.
  • The legs can also be separated into drumsticks and thighs. These can be used skin-on or off.
  • Thigh meat can be processed for stir-fry’s or flattened and crumbed. Diced thigh meat is used for stews.
  • The breast or sûpreme is removed from the whole bird by removing the wishbone and then following the carcass through the wing joint. The skin can be left on or removed. The wing bone can also be left on or removed.
  • Breast meat is perfect for stir-fry’s, schnitzel, Kiev or escalope
